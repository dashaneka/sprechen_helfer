You get a card with a theme (Thema) and keyword (Stichwort) and must form a question.

The 2 Main Question Types

1. W-Fragen (for information)
Structure: W-Wort + Verb + Sie + ... ?
Example: Wo wohnen Sie?
2. Ja/Nein-Fragen (for a yes/no answer)
Structure: Verb + Sie + ... ?
Example: Haben Sie Kinder?

The 3 Missing Ingredients for PERFECT Questions

Ingredient 1: "Power Combos" (Verb + Noun Pairs)
Some verbs and nouns almost always go together. Learn them as a pair!
  • Sport machen (to do sport)
  • Musik hören (to listen to music)
  • Deutsch lernen (to learn German)
  • einkaufen gehen (to go shopping)
Ingredient 2: The Right "Little Words" (Prepositions)
Using the correct small word is crucial.
  • am: For days of the week & the weekend (am Montag, am Wochenende).
  • um: For specific clock times (um 8 Uhr).
  • in: For places and general concepts (in Nepal, in der Freizeit).
Ingredient 3: The "Einen" Trick (Accusative Case)
When you use an "action" verb like haben, kaufen, lesen, möchten, sehen, the masculine article changes.
  • der changes to den
  • ein changes to einen
  • Feminine (die) and neuter (das) articles do not change.
  • Example: Haben Sie einen Stift? (because it's der Stift)

You see a picture card and must make a polite request (eine Bitte).

The 2 Ways to Make a Request

Method 1: The Polite Question (Recommended)
Structure: Können Sie mir/bitte + ... + Verb (at the end)?
Example: Können Sie mir bitte den Stift geben?
Method 2: The Formal Command (Imperativ)
Structure: Verb + Sie + bitte ...
Example: Geben Sie mir bitte den Stift.

How to React to a Request

Positive Reactions (Saying Yes)
Ja, natürlich. | Ja, gern. | Hier, bitte.
Negative Reactions (Saying No)
Tut mir leid, das geht nicht. | Entschuldigung, ich habe keinen...

Special Cases

Negative Requests (e.g., "No Smoking")
The phrase is usually bitte nicht + [verb].
Example: Können Sie bitte hier nicht rauchen?
Separable Verbs (e.g., zumachen)
With können, the verb becomes whole again at the end of the sentence.
Example: Können Sie bitte das Fenster zumachen?

Knowing the correct article (der, die, das) is the final key. The best method is to learn the noun and its article together as one word.

Trick 1: Identify by Word Ending

  • It's probably die (feminine) if it ends in: -e, -ung, -heit, -keit, -schaft, -ei.
  • It's probably das (neuter) if it ends in: -chen, -lein or is a verb used as a noun (das Essen).

Trick 2: Identify by Word Meaning

  • It's probably der (masculine) if it is: a male person/profession, day, month, season, or weather.
  • It's probably die (feminine) if it is: a female person/profession.
